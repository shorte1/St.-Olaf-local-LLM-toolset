Prompt: A bat and a ball cost $1.10 in total. The bat costs $1.00 more than the ball. How much does the ball cost? Show your working.
========================================
Let's denote:

- \( \text{Ball Cost} = x \)
- \( \text{Bat Cost} = y \)

According to the problem, we have two pieces of information:

1. **Total Cost:**
   \[
   x + y = 1.10
   \]

2. **Difference in Cost:**
   \[
   y = x + 1.00
   \]

**Step 1:** Substitute the second equation into the first.

\[
x + (x + 1.00) = 1.10
\]

**Step 2:** Simplify and solve for \( x \).

\[
2x + 1.00 = 1.10 \\
2x = 1.10 - 1.00 \\
2x = 0.10 \\
x = \frac{0.10}{2} \\
x = 0.05
\]

**Step 3:** Find the cost of the bat using \( y = x + 1.00 \).

\[
y = 0.05 + 1.00 = 1.05
\]

**Final Answer:**

The ball costs \(\boxed{0.05}\) dollars, and the bat costs \(\boxed{1.05}\) dollars.

However, if we focus solely on the cost of the ball as requested:

\[
\boxed{0.05}
\]
