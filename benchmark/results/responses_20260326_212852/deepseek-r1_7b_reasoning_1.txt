Prompt: A bat and a ball cost $1.10 in total. The bat costs $1.00 more than the ball. How much does the ball cost? Show your working.
========================================
**Solution:**

Let's denote:
- Let \( b \) be the cost of the ball in dollars.

Given that the bat costs **\$1.00 more** than the ball, we can express the cost of the bat as:
\[ \text{Cost of the bat} = b + 1 \]

The total cost of the bat and the ball is **\$1.10**, so we can write the equation:
\[
b + (b + 1) = 1.10
\]

**Step 1:** Combine like terms.
\[
2b + 1 = 1.10
\]

**Step 2:** Subtract 1 from both sides to isolate the term with \( b \).
\[
2b = 1.10 - 1
\]
\[
2b = 0.10
\]

**Step 3:** Divide both sides by 2 to solve for \( b \).
\[
b = \frac{0.10}{2}
\]
\[
b = 0.05
\]

Therefore, the cost of the ball is:
\[
\boxed{\$0.05}
\]
