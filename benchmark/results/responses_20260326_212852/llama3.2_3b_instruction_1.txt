========================================
Autumn Haiku:

Golden leaves fall slow
Crisp air whispers of decay
Summer's final sigh

French Translation:
Feuilles d'automne:

Folles dorées tombent
Air sec murmure de décadence
Sifflement de l'hiver

Seasonal Symbolism:

* "Golden leaves fall slow" symbolizes the gradual decline of summer and the beginning of autumn. The golden color represents the warmth and vitality of summer, which is slowly fading away.
* "Crisp air whispers of decay" evokes the feeling of a chilly breeze that carries the scent of rotting leaves and decaying vegetation. This symbolizes the inevitability of death and the end of life's cycles.
* "Summer's final sigh" serves as a poignant reminder of the passing of time and the fleeting nature of life. The word "sigh" conveys a sense of melancholy and resignation, emphasizing the sadness that accompanies the departure of summer.

The French translation maintains the same imagery and symbolism as the original haiku, conveying the same autumnal themes in a more lyrical and poetic way.
