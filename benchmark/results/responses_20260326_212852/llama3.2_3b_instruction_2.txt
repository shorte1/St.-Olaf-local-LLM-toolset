========================================
Here are the planets in our solar system listed in order of size from largest to smallest, along with their diameters in kilometers and whether they have rings:

1. Jupiter - Diameter: 142,984 km (has no solid rings, but has a ring system composed of dust and debris)
2. Saturn - Diameter: 116,460 km (has prominent ring system)
3. Uranus - Diameter: 51,118 km (has ring system)
4. Neptune - Diameter: 49,528 km (has ring system)
5. Earth - Diameter: 12,742 km
6. Venus - Diameter: 12,104 km
7. Mars - Diameter: 6,794 km
8. Mercury - Diameter: 4,879 km

Note that the size of a planet can be measured in different ways, including its diameter (the distance across its equator) and its mass (its total amount of matter). The diameters listed above are generally accepted values for each planet.

Also, it's worth noting that the gas giants (Jupiter and Saturn) have ring systems that are composed mostly of ice particles and rock debris, while the ice giants (Uranus and Neptune) have more prominent ring systems.
