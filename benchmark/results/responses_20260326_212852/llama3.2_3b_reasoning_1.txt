Prompt: A bat and a ball cost $1.10 in total. The bat costs $1.00 more than the ball. How much does the ball cost? Show your working.
========================================
To solve this problem, we can start by using variables to represent the unknown quantities.

Let x be the cost of the ball.

Since the bat costs $1.00 more than the ball, the cost of the bat is x + $1.00.

The total cost of the bat and the ball together is $1.10, so we can set up an equation:

x + (x + $1.00) = $1.10

Combine like terms:

2x + $1.00 = $1.10

Subtract $1.00 from both sides:

2x = $0.10

Divide both sides by 2:

x = $0.05

So, the ball costs $0.05.
