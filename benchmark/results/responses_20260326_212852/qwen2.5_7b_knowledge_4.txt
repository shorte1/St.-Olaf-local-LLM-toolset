Prompt: What were the main causes and consequences of the 2008 financial crisis?
========================================
The 2008 global financial crisis was one of the most significant economic events in modern history, leading to severe impacts on economies worldwide. Several factors contributed to its onset, while various consequences followed.

### Main Causes:

1. **Subprime Mortgage Lending**: Banks and mortgage lenders provided loans to borrowers with poor credit ratings, leading to a high number of defaults as these individuals struggled to pay back their loans.
2. **Securitization and Derivatives**: Financial institutions packaged subprime mortgages into complex financial instruments such as mortgage-backed securities (MBS) and collateralized debt obligations (CDOs), which were sold to investors worldwide. When many of these investments failed, the value of the entire market was severely impacted.
3. **Lax Regulation and Supervision**: Weak oversight allowed for risky practices in the financial sector, including inadequate risk assessment models and loose lending standards.
4. **Housing Bubble**: An unsustainable rise in housing prices led to an imbalance between home values and borrowers' ability to pay, eventually bursting when the market corrected itself.
5. **Global Interconnectedness**: The crisis spread rapidly due to global financial interdependence, as many countries held investments in U.S. mortgage-backed securities.

### Main Consequences:

1. **Economic Recession**: Many economies worldwide experienced significant recessions with high unemployment rates and lower economic growth.
2. **Bank Bailouts**: Governments intervened by bailing out major banks that were considered "too big to fail," leading to public concerns over the use of taxpayer money in financial markets.
3. **Increased Regulation**: The crisis led to a wave of new regulations aimed at preventing future crises, such as the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act in the United States.
4. **Public Sector Debt**: Governments incurred substantial debt from rescue packages and stimulus measures implemented during the crisis.
5. **Financial Market Reforms**: There was a shift towards more conservative financial practices, with increased focus on risk management and transparency within financial institutions.

The 2008 financial crisis had profound impacts that extended beyond just the economic sphere, influencing politics, public sentiment towards banks and governments, and international cooperation in financial regulation.
