Prompt: A bat and a ball cost $1.10 in total. The bat costs $1.00 more than the ball. How much does the ball cost? Show your working.
========================================
Let's define the variables first:
- Let \( x \) be the cost of the ball.
- Therefore, the cost of the bat would be \( x + 1.00 \).

According to the problem, the total cost of the bat and the ball is $1.10. So we can write the equation as:
\[ x + (x + 1.00) = 1.10 \]

Simplify and solve for \( x \):
\[ 2x + 1.00 = 1.10 \]
Subtract 1.00 from both sides:
\[ 2x = 0.10 \]
Divide by 2:
\[ x = 0.05 \]

So, the ball costs $0.05 (or 5 cents).
