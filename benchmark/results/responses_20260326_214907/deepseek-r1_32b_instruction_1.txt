Prompt: Write a haiku about autumn, then translate it to French, then explain the seasonal symbolism used.
========================================
**Haiku:**

Leaves whisper goodbye,  
A river flows softly by,  
Seasons shift slowly.

**Translation in French:**

Feuilles murmurent adieu,  
Une rivière coule doucement,  
Les saisons changent lentement.

**Explanation of Seasonal Symbolism:**

1. **"Leaves whisper goodbye"**: This line symbolizes the end of summer and the transition into autumn. The falling leaves are a common sign of this season, representing the natural cycle of life and death. The use of "whisper" gives a sense of quiet and gentle change, emphasizing the serene yet significant shift in nature.

2. **"A river flows softly by"**: The river represents time passing and continuity. In autumn, the flow of water mirrors the gentle but inevitable progression of the seasons. It conveys a sense of calmness and the ongoing movement of life, even as things around change with the season.

3. **"Seasons shift slowly"**: This line directly addresses the gradual transition from one season to another. It highlights the slow and methodical process of nature's cycles, reinforcing themes of transformation and time. The word "slowly" underscores patience and the quiet strength of natural processes.

Together, these elements create a haiku that captures the essence of autumn through imagery of change, reflection, and the passage of time, all while adhering to the traditional 5-7-5 syllable structure.
