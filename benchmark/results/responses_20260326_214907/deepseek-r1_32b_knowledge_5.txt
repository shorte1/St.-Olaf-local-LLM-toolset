**TCP vs. UDP: A Comprehensive Overview**

**Transmission Control Protocol (TCP):**
- **Connection-Oriented:** Establishes a connection before data transfer, ensuring all packets are received in order.
- **Reliable:** Guarantees delivery of all packets, making it suitable for applications where data integrity is crucial, such as web browsing and email.
- **Features:**
  - Three-way handshake to establish connections, adding overhead but ensuring readiness.
  - Handles flow control and congestion, adjusting data transmission based on network conditions.

**User Datagram Protocol (UDP):**
- **Connectionless:** Sends data without establishing a connection, prioritizing speed over reliability.
- **Unreliable:** Does not ensure delivery or order of packets, ideal for real-time applications like streaming and online gaming where speed is paramount.

**Choosing Between TCP and UDP:**
- Use **TCP** when guaranteed delivery, ordered transmission, and error checking are essential (e.g., file transfers, messaging).
- Opt for **UDP** in scenarios requiring quick data transfer, even with potential packet loss, such as real-time video conferencing or live streams.

In summary, TCP is reliable and orderly but slower, while UDP is fast and efficient yet less reliable. The choice depends on the application's needs for speed versus data integrity.
