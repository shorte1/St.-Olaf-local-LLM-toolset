Prompt: Three friends split a restaurant bill. The bill is $30 so they each pay $10. The waiter realizes the bill was only $25 and returns $5. The friends each take $1 and tip the waiter $2. They each paid $9, totaling $27, plus the $2 tip is $29. Where is the missing dollar? Explain the flaw in this reasoning.
========================================
The confusion arises from how the totals are being added after the refund and tip. Let's break it down step by step:

1. **Initial Payment:**
   - Total Bill: \$30
   - Each Friend Pays: \$10
   - Total Collected: 3 × \$10 = \$30

2. **Realization of Error:**
   - Actual Bill Amount: \$25
   - Overpayment: \$30 - \$25 = \$5

3. **Refund and Tip Distribution:**
   - Refund Returned to Friends: \$3 (each friend gets \$1)
   - Tip Given to Waiter: \$2

4. **Correct Total Calculation:**
   - The total money should account for the actual bill, the tip, and the refund.
   - Total Bill + Tip + Refund = \$25 + \$2 + \$3 = \$30

**Where is the missing dollar?**

The error in the original reasoning comes from adding the amount paid by friends (\$27) and the tip (\$2), resulting in \$29. However, this approach double-counts the tip since the tip is already included in the total amount collected.

**Correct Statement:**
- Each friend effectively paid \$9 (after refund).
- Total Payment from Friends: 3 × \$9 = \$27
- This \$27 covers:
  - Actual Bill: \$25
  - Tip to Waiter: \$2

Thus, there is no missing dollar. The initial reasoning incorrectly added the tip to the reduced payment (\$27), leading to confusion.

\boxed{30}
