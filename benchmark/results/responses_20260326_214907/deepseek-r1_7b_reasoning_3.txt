Prompt: A lily pad doubles in size every day. It takes 48 days to cover a lake. How long does it take to cover half the lake? Show your working.
========================================
Given that a lily pad doubles in size every day and takes 48 days to cover a lake, we need to determine how long it takes to cover half the lake.

1. The growth of the lily pad is exponential, with its area doubling each day.
2. Let \( A(t) \) be the area covered by the lily pad on day \( t \). The formula for this growth is:
   \[
   A(t) = A_0 \times 2^t
   \]
   where \( A_0 \) is the initial area.
3. On day 48, the lily pad covers the entire lake, so:
   \[
   L = A_0 \times 2^{48}
   \]
4. We need to find when the lily pad covered half the lake. Let \( t_x \) be the day it covers half the lake:
   \[
   A(48 - x) = A_0 \times 2^{48 - x} = \frac{1}{2}L
   \]
5. Substituting \( L = A_0 \times 2^{48} \) into the equation:
   \[
   A_0 \times 2^{48 - x} = \frac{1}{2} \times (A_0 \times 2^{48})
   \]
6. Simplifying both sides by canceling \( A_0 \):
   \[
   2^{48 - x} = \frac{1}{2} \times 2^{48}
   \]
7. Further simplification gives:
   \[
   2^{48 - x} = 2^{47}
   \]
8. Equating the exponents since the bases are equal:
   \[
   48 - x = 47
   \]
9. Solving for \( x \):
   \[
   x = 1
   \]

Thus, it takes 47 days to cover half the lake.
