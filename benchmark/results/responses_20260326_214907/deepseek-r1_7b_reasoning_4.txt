Prompt: Three friends split a restaurant bill. The bill is $30 so they each pay $10. The waiter realizes the bill was only $25 and returns $5. The friends each take $1 and tip the waiter $2. They each paid $9, totaling $27, plus the $2 tip is $29. Where is the missing dollar? Explain the flaw in this reasoning.
========================================
The classic riddle involves three friends splitting a restaurant bill of $25. Here's the breakdown:

1. The total bill was $25.
2. Each friend paid $10, totaling $30.
3. The waiter realizes the bill was only $25 and returns $5 to the friends.
4. The friends each take $1 back, totaling $3, and tip the waiter $2.

The confusion arises when trying to add the amounts incorrectly:

- Each friend paid $9 in total ($10 - $1).
- Three friends paying $9 each totals $27.
- Adding the $2 tip results in $29, which seems incorrect because the bill was only $25.

However, the correct accounting is as follows:

- The initial payment of $30 by the friends.
- The waiter returns $5, reducing the total to $25 (the actual bill).
- The remaining $2 is kept as a tip by the waiter.

Thus, the breakdown is:
- Bill: $25
- Return to friends: $3
- Tip: $2

Adding these together gives the correct total of $30. There is no missing dollar; it was incorrectly accounted for in the flawed reasoning.

\boxed{There is no missing dollar}
