Prompt: Write a haiku about autumn, then translate it to French, then explain the seasonal symbolism used.
========================================
## English Haiku:

Crimson leaves descend,
Whispering on the cool breeze,
Winter slumbers near.

## French Translation:

Feuilles rouges tombent,
Murmurant sur la brise fraîche,
L'hiver sommeille près.


## Seasonal Symbolism Explained:

* **"Crimson leaves descend"**:  This line evokes the quintessential image of autumn - vibrant red and orange leaves falling from trees. This symbolizes the natural cycle of decay and death that precedes winter.
* **"Whispering on the cool breeze"**: The sound of leaves rustling in the wind adds to the melancholic atmosphere often associated with autumn. The "cool breeze" further emphasizes the shift towards colder temperatures.

* **"Winter slumbers near"**: This line hints at the impending arrival of winter, a season of dormancy and stillness.  The use of "slumbers" suggests that winter is not yet fully awake but is slowly approaching.



The haiku uses simple imagery to capture the essence of autumn as a transitional season marked by fading beauty, falling leaves, and the anticipation of winter's rest.
