Prompt: Write a formal email declining a job offer, then rewrite it as a casual text message, then rewrite it as a Shakespearean monologue.
========================================
## Formal Email:

Subject: Job Offer - [Your Name]

Dear [Hiring Manager Name],

Thank you very much for offering me the position of [Position Name] at [Company Name]. I appreciate you taking the time to interview me and share more about this exciting opportunity.

After careful consideration, I have decided to decline the offer at this time. This was a difficult decision as I was impressed with [Company Name] and the team. However, [Briefly state your reason for declining - e.g., another opportunity aligns better with my current career goals].

I wish you all the best in finding a suitable candidate for the role. 

Sincerely,
[Your Name]



## Casual Text Message:

Hey [Hiring Manager Name], thanks again for the offer! I really appreciated learning more about the position. Ultimately, I've decided to go in a different direction. Best of luck with the search!


## Shakespearean Monologue:

Hark, noble sir, thy generous offer stands,
A tempting prize, with gilded hands it lands.
Yet, though my heart doth flutter at the thought,
Of joining ranks with talents finely wrought,
Fate's fickle hand doth guide me down a lane,
Where other goals and aspirations reign.

Thus, with regret that stings like winter's chill,
I must decline thy offer, good sir still.
Know that my thanks are true, my spirit bold,
But destiny doth call, a tale unfolds.
