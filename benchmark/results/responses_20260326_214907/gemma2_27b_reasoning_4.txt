Prompt: Three friends split a restaurant bill. The bill is $30 so they each pay $10. The waiter realizes the bill was only $25 and returns $5. The friends each take $1 and tip the waiter $2. They each paid $9, totaling $27, plus the $2 tip is $29. Where is the missing dollar? Explain the flaw in this reasoning.
========================================
This is a classic riddle that plays on how we frame the math problem. Here's where the thinking goes wrong:

**The mistake is adding the $2 tip to the $27 the friends effectively paid.**  

Here's a clearer breakdown:

* **The bill:** $25
* **What the friends paid:** $30 (3 x $10)
* **Change received:** $5 

**Here's how the money actually flows:**

* The friends get $3 back ($1 each).
* They give the waiter $2.
* This means $25 (the bill) + $2 (tip) = $27 was accounted for.


The $27 represents the total cost of the meal and the tip.  There's no missing dollar.
