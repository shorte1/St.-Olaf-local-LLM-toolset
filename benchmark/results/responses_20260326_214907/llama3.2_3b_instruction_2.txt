========================================
Here are the planets in our solar system listed by size from largest to smallest, along with their diameters in kilometers, and marked which ones have rings:

1. Jupiter - 142,984 km (no rings)
2. Saturn - 116,464 km (yes, has rings)
3. Uranus - 51,118 km (no rings)
4. Neptune - 49,528 km (no rings)
5. Earth - 12,742 km (yes, has natural satellites and no artificial rings)
6. Venus - 12,104 km (no rings)
7. Mars - 6,794 km (no rings)
8. Mercury - 4,879 km (no rings)

Note: The sizes of the planets can vary slightly due to their ellipsoidal shapes and atmospheric thickness, but these values are generally accepted.

Also, it's worth noting that while Jupiter and Saturn have extensive ring systems, the other gas giants (Uranus and Neptune) also have much thinner ring systems.
