Prompt: Write a formal email declining a job offer, then rewrite it as a casual text message, then rewrite it as a Shakespearean monologue.
========================================
**Formal Email**

Subject: Declining Job Offer for [Position]

Dear [Hiring Manager's Name],

I am writing to express my sincere gratitude for the job offer extended to me for the position of [Position] at [Company Name]. I was thrilled to learn about this opportunity and appreciate the time you took to speak with me about the role.

However, after careful consideration, I regret to inform you that I have decided to decline the offer. While I am excited about the company's mission and values, I have chosen to pursue another opportunity that aligns more closely with my career goals and personal aspirations.

Please know that my decision is in no way a reflection on the company or the position itself. I appreciate the professionalism and courtesy shown during our conversations, and I wish the organization continued success in finding a suitable candidate.

Thank you again for the offer, and I wish you all the best in your search.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]

**Casual Text Message**

"Hey [Hiring Manager's Name], just wanted to say thanks for the job offer, but I've decided to go with another opportunity. Not a bad fit for me, hope you find someone great for the role! Best"

**Shakespearean Monologue**

"Oh, fairest employer, thou hast extended unto me
A token of thy esteem, a badge of honor's might.
'Twas a call to join thy noble company,
And don my cloak with dignity and light.

But, alas, fair sir, I must decline thy offer's sweet
Invitation. For though thy words were honeyed and bright,
My heart doth beat for another pursuit, a path
That beckons me to follow where it shall lead in flight.

Pray, know that my rejection hath no ill intent,
For thou art a company of great virtue and bent.
Thy mission and values shine like beacons bright,
Guiding thee towards excellence, shining with delight.

Farewell, fair sir, may Fortune smile upon thy way,
And may another candidate's star rise to seize the day."
