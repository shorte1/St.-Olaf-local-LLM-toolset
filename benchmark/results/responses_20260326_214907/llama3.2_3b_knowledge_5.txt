Prompt: Explain the difference between TCP and UDP protocols and when you would use each.
========================================
TCP (Transmission Control Protocol) and UDP (User Datagram Protocol) are two fundamental protocols used for communication over the internet. While both protocols are used for data transfer, they have distinct differences in their design, functionality, and use cases.

**TCP:**

TCP is a connection-oriented protocol that provides reliable, error-checked, and sequential delivery of data between devices. It establishes a connection between the sender and receiver before sending data, and it ensures that data is delivered in the correct order.

Here are some key characteristics of TCP:

1. **Reliability**: TCP ensures that data is delivered accurately and in the correct order.
2. **Error checking**: TCP performs error-checking to detect and correct errors during transmission.
3. **Connection establishment**: TCP establishes a connection between the sender and receiver before sending data.
4. **Flow control**: TCP regulates the amount of data sent by both the sender and receiver.

**UDP:**

UDP is a connectionless protocol that provides best-effort delivery of data, with no guarantee of error-checking or reliability. It does not establish a connection between devices before sending data, and it allows for faster transmission times.

Here are some key characteristics of UDP:

1. **Best effort**: UDP ensures that data is delivered as quickly as possible, but there's no guarantee it will arrive correctly.
2. **No error checking**: UDP does not perform error-checking, so errors during transmission cannot be corrected.
3. **Connectionless**: UDP does not establish a connection between devices before sending data.
4. **Faster transmission**: UDP allows for faster transmission times since it doesn't require the overhead of establishing and maintaining a connection.

**When to use each protocol:**

1. **TCP:** Use TCP for applications that require reliable, sequential delivery of data, such as:
 * File transfers
 * Email
 * Web browsing
 * Online banking
2. **UDP:** Use UDP for applications that prioritize speed over reliability, such as:
 * Online gaming (e.g., video games, multiplayer)
 * Real-time streaming (e.g., video conferencing, live sports)
 * VoIP (Voice over Internet Protocol) calls
 * DNS resolution

In summary, TCP is used for applications that require reliable and sequential data delivery, while UDP is used for applications that prioritize speed over reliability.
