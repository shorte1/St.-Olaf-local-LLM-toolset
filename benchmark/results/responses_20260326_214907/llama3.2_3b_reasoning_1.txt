Prompt: A bat and a ball cost $1.10 in total. The bat costs $1.00 more than the ball. How much does the ball cost? Show your working.
========================================
Let's say the cost of the ball is x.

Since the bat costs $1.00 more than the ball, we can set up an equation to represent this:

Bat = Ball + 1
or
x + 1 = Bat

We know that the total cost of the bat and ball together is $1.10. So we can write another equation:

Bat + Ball = 1.10
Substituting x for the ball, we get:
(x + 1) + x = 1.10

Combine like terms:
2x + 1 = 1.10

Subtract 1 from both sides:
2x = 0.10

Divide by 2:
x = 0.05

The cost of the ball is $0.05 or 5 cents.
