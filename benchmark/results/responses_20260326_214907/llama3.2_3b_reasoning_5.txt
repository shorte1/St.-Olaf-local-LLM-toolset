Prompt: You have two ropes that each take exactly one hour to burn, but they burn unevenly. How do you measure exactly 45 minutes using only these ropes and a lighter?
========================================
This is a classic puzzle! Here's the solution:

1. Light both ropes simultaneously.
2. When one rope has burned for 30 minutes (i.e., it's halfway burnt), extinguish the flame under that rope.
3. At this point, both ropes will have burnt for 30 minutes each.
4. Extinguish the flame under the second rope as well.

Now you have a situation where:

- The first rope has burned for another 15 minutes (total of 45 minutes).
- The second rope has burned for only 0 minutes (since it was extinguished).

By using this technique, you've effectively measured exactly 45 minutes with two ropes that each take one hour to burn.
