Prompt: A bat and a ball cost $1.10 in total. The bat costs $1.00 more than the ball. How much does the ball cost? Show your working.
========================================
 Let's call the cost of the ball \( b \) dollars. According to the problem, the bat costs \( 1.00 \) dollar more than the ball, so the cost of the bat is \( b + 1.00 \) dollars.

The total cost of both items is given as \( 1.10 \) dollars. So we can write the equation:

\[ b + (b + 1.00) = 1.10 \]

Now, let's solve for \( b \):

\[ 2b + 1.00 = 1.10 \]
\[ 2b = 1.10 - 1.00 \]
\[ 2b = 0.10 \]
\[ b = 0.10 / 2 \]
\[ b = 0.05 \]

So, the ball costs \( 0.05 \) dollars, or 5 cents.
