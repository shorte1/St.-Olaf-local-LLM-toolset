Prompt: Read the following paragraph and identify: 1) the main argument, 2) any logical fallacies, 3) what evidence would strengthen the argument. Paragraph: Everyone I know prefers working from home, so it is clear that remote work is more productive than office work. Companies that force employees to come in are clearly behind the times and will lose their best talent. Studies showing office work is better are funded by real estate companies and cannot be trusted.
========================================
 1) The main argument is that remote work is more productive than office work, based on the observation that everyone known by the author prefers working from home.

2) The logical fallacy in this paragraph is anecdotal evidence (also known as appeal to personal experience or hasty generalization). While it's valuable to consider personal experiences, they should not be the sole basis for a broad argument without supporting additional evidence.

3) To strengthen the argument, more robust evidence would help. This could include:
- Data from larger and more diverse samples of workers who have experience with both remote work and office work.
- Studies or reports comparing productivity levels between remote work and office work, ideally not funded by real estate companies.
- Testimonials or data from companies that have successfully implemented remote work policies and maintained high productivity levels.
- Metrics on employee satisfaction, engagement, and retention rates in both remote and office environments.
