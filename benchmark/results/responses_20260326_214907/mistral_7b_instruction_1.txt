Prompt: Write a haiku about autumn, then translate it to French, then explain the seasonal symbolism used.
========================================
 Original Haiku (English):

Crimson leaves fall,
Whispers of summer's end,
Autumn's dance begins.

Traduction en français :

Feuilles de couleur cramée tombent,
Souffles d'une fin d'été,
Danse automnale commence.

Explanation:
The haiku is a form of traditional Japanese poetry that captures the essence of seasons. This particular piece captures the symbolism associated with autumn. The "crimson leaves" symbolize the vibrant colors and changing foliage that characterize the season in many regions. The "whispers of summer's end" suggest a transition from warm, lively days to cooler, more tranquil ones. Lastly, "Autumn's dance begins" encapsulates the sense of movement and change typical of this season as leaves fall and the natural world prepares for winter. In French, the translation maintains the imagery and rhythm of the original haiku.
