Prompt: Write a haiku about autumn, then translate it to French, then explain the seasonal symbolism used.
========================================
Haiku:
Leaves whisper in gold,
Crisp air holds secrets of smoke—
Sunset paints paths brown.

French translation:
Feuilles murmurent en or,
L'air frais tient des secrets de fumée —
Le coucher du soleil peint les chemins brun.

Explanation of seasonal symbolism used:

- **Gold Leaves:** The color gold is a hallmark of autumn, symbolizing the changing leaves as they transition from green to vibrant shades like yellow and orange. This change signifies the end of summer and the onset of cooler weather.

- **Crisp Air Holds Secrets of Smoke:** Autumn often brings with it the crispness in the air due to the drop in temperature and humidity. The "secrets of smoke" could symbolize bonfires or the burning leaves, both common activities in autumn as people gather for outdoor events like harvest festivals and Halloween celebrations.

- **Sunset Paints Paths Brown:** As autumn progresses, not only do the trees change color but also the ground becomes covered with fallen leaves, turning paths a rich brown. This imagery represents the finality of summer and the approach of winter, where the landscape prepares itself for dormancy.
