Prompt: A bat and a ball cost $1.10 in total. The bat costs $1.00 more than the ball. How much does the ball cost? Show your working.
========================================
To solve this problem, let's use algebra to set up an equation based on the information given.

Let \( x \) be the cost of the ball in dollars.
According to the problem:
- The bat costs $1.00 more than the ball, so the bat costs \( x + 1.00 \).
- Together, they cost a total of $1.10.

We can write this as an equation:

\[ x + (x + 1.00) = 1.10 \]

Now, combine like terms:

\[ 2x + 1.00 = 1.10 \]

Next, subtract 1.00 from both sides of the equation to isolate \( 2x \):

\[ 2x = 0.10 \]

Finally, divide both sides by 2 to solve for \( x \):

\[ x = 0.05 \]

So, the ball costs $0.05 (or 5 cents).
