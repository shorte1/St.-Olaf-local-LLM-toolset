Sure! Here is the list of the planets in our solar system ordered by size (diameter) from largest to smallest, along with their diameters in kilometers and a note on whether they have rings:

1. **Jupiter** - Diameter: approximately 139,820 km - Jupiter has multiple prominent rings.
   
2. **Saturn** - Diameter: approximately 116,460 km - Saturn is well-known for its beautiful ring system.

3. **Uranus** - Diameter: approximately 50,724 km - Uranus also has a ring system, though it's less prominent than those of Jupiter and Saturn.
   
4. **Neptune** - Diameter: approximately 49,244 km - Neptune has a faint ring system.

5. **Earth** - Diameter: approximately 12,742 km

6. **Mars** - Diameter: approximately 6,779 km

7. **Juno** (this is not a planet but for context, it's the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter which contains many small bodies)

8. **Venus** - Diameter: approximately 12,104 km

9. **Merkury** (this is not a planet but for context, it's the closest to the Sun with no significant atmosphere or rings)

10. **Pluto** - Diameter: approximately 2,377 km - Pluto was reclassified as a dwarf planet and does not have rings.

11. **Ceres** - Diameter: approximately 945 km - Ceres is another dwarf planet located in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter; it also lacks rings.

Note that while Earth and Venus do not have visible rings, some of the moons around these planets can have their own ring systems (e.g., Saturn's moon Titan has a thin haze layer, but no true rings).
