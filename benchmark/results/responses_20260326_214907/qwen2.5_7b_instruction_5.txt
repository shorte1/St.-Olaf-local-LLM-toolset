Prompt: Write a formal email declining a job offer, then rewrite it as a casual text message, then rewrite it as a Shakespearean monologue.
========================================
**Formal Email:**
Subject: Declining Job Offer

Dear [Hiring Manager's Name],

Thank you for the opportunity to consider the position of [Position] at [Company Name]. After careful consideration and review of all aspects, including the role responsibilities and career goals, I have decided to decline your offer. While the role presented an excellent opportunity that aligned with my professional interests, I believe there are other avenues where I can contribute more effectively.

Thank you once again for the confidence placed in me. I appreciate the time and consideration given during this process.

Warm regards,

[Your Name]

---

**Casual Text Message:**
Hey [Hiring Manager's Name],

Thanks for the job offer, but I'm going to have to pass on it. The role was cool, but I think there are better matches out there for me right now. Appreciate you considering me!

Take care,
[Your Name]

---

**Shakespearean Monologue:**
Good [Hiring Manager's Name], in the realm of my noble heart and aspirations, a bountiful offer hath been laid before me—yea, an opportunity to join thine esteemed company as [Position] at [Company Name]. Yet, as I pondered upon this gift with due deliberation, it came to pass that certain considerations did weigh heavy in my soul. 

It is true, the role presented itself as a noble path, yet I must needs confess that it doth not align perfectly with mine own ambitions and wherefore I find myself compelled to decline thine generous offer. There be many other fields where mine abilities may serve more fittingly, as I seek out new horizons wherein I can better contribute.

Therefore, in this moment of parting, I extend my thanks for the honor you have bestowed upon me. Though my path now lies elsewhere, your company's name shall remain dear to my heart. Farewell!

[Your Name]
