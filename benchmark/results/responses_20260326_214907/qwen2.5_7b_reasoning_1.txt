Prompt: A bat and a ball cost $1.10 in total. The bat costs $1.00 more than the ball. How much does the ball cost? Show your working.
========================================
Let's denote the cost of the ball as \( x \) dollars.

Given that the bat costs $1.00 more than the ball, we can express the cost of the bat as \( x + 1 \) dollars.

The total cost of both items is given as $1.10. Therefore, we can set up the following equation:

\[ x + (x + 1) = 1.10 \]

Simplifying the left side gives us:

\[ 2x + 1 = 1.10 \]

To solve for \( x \), first subtract 1 from both sides of the equation:

\[ 2x = 0.10 \]

Next, divide both sides by 2:

\[ x = 0.05 \]

So, the ball costs $0.05.

We can verify this solution by checking that the bat's cost is indeed $1 more than the ball's cost and that their total cost is $1.10:

- Cost of the ball: \( 0.05 \) dollars
- Cost of the bat: \( 0.05 + 1 = 1.05 \) dollars

Total cost:
\[ 0.05 + 1.05 = 1.10 \]

This confirms that our solution is correct. The ball costs $0.05.
