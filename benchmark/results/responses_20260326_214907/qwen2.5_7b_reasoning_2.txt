Prompt: If it takes 5 machines 5 minutes to make 5 widgets, how long would it take 100 machines to make 100 widgets? Show your working.
========================================
To solve this problem, let's break down the information given and apply logical reasoning step by step.

### Given:
- **5 machines** take **5 minutes** to make **5 widgets**.

From this, we can deduce that each machine makes 1 widget in 5 minutes.

Now, consider the scenario with **100 machines** making **100 widgets**:

1. **Determine the production rate per machine:**
   - Each of the 5 machines produces 1 widget in 5 minutes.
   - Therefore, each machine's production rate is \( \frac{1 \text{ widget}}{5 \text{ minutes}} \).

2. **Scale up to 100 machines:**
   - If we have 100 machines and each machine can produce 1 widget in 5 minutes, then:
     - The 100 machines working together will also produce 100 widgets.
     - Since the number of machines is directly proportional to the number of widgets produced, all 100 machines would collectively produce 100 widgets in the same amount of time that one machine produces one widget.

3. **Conclusion:**
   - It would still take \(5\) minutes for 100 machines to make 100 widgets because each of the 100 machines is producing its own widget simultaneously, and all are working at the same rate.

Therefore, it would take **5 minutes** for 100 machines to make 100 widgets.
