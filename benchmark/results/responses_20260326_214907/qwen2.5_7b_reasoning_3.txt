Prompt: A lily pad doubles in size every day. It takes 48 days to cover a lake. How long does it take to cover half the lake? Show your working.
========================================
To determine how long it takes for the lily pad to cover half of the lake, we can work backwards from the point where the lily pad covers the entire lake.

Given:
- The lily pad doubles in size every day.
- It takes 48 days to cover the whole lake.

Let's denote:
- \( L \) as the full coverage of the lake by the lily pad.
- \( \frac{L}{2} \) as half the coverage of the lake by the lily pad.

Since the lily pad doubles in size every day, on the 48th day it covers the whole lake (\( L \)). Therefore, the previous day (the 47th day), it must have covered half the lake (\( \frac{L}{2} \)), because doubling \( \frac{L}{2} \) results in \( L \).

So, it takes **47 days** to cover half the lake.

Here's a simple summary of the working:
- Day 48: Full coverage (L)
- Day 47: Half coverage (\(\frac{L}{2}\))

Therefore, the lily pad covers half the lake in \(\boxed{47}\) days.
