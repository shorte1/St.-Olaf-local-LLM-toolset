Prompt: A bat and a ball cost $1.10 in total. The bat costs $1.00 more than the ball. How much does the ball cost? Show your working.
========================================
Let's solve the problem step by step.

**Given:**
- Total cost of the bat and ball: \$1.10
- The bat costs \$1.00 more than the ball.

**Step 1:** Let’s denote the cost of the **ball** as \( x \) dollars.

**Step 2:** Since the bat costs \$1.00 more than the ball, the **bat's cost** can be expressed as:
\[
\text{Bat} = x + 1
\]

**Step 3:** According to the problem, the total cost of both items is \$1.10. Therefore, we can set up the equation:
\[
x \ (\text{Ball}) + (x + 1) \ (\text{Bat}) = 1.10
\]

**Step 4:** Combine like terms:
\[
2x + 1 = 1.10
\]

**Step 5:** Subtract \$1 from both sides to solve for \( x \):
\[
2x = 0.10
\]

**Step 6:** Divide both sides by 2 to find the value of \( x \):
\[
x = \frac{0.10}{2} = 0.05
\]

**Conclusion:**
The **ball costs \$0.05**.

\[
\boxed{\$0.05}
