Prompt: A bat and a ball cost $1.10 in total. The bat costs $1.00 more than the ball. How much does the ball cost? Show your working.
========================================
Let's solve the problem step-by-step.

**Given:**
- A bat and a ball together cost \$1.10.
- The bat costs \$1.00 more than the ball.

**Let’s define:**
- Let \( x \) be the cost of the **ball** in dollars.
- Then, the cost of the **bat** would be \( x + 1 \) (since it's \$1.00 more than the ball).

**Set up the equation based on the total cost:**
\[
\text{Cost of Ball} + \text{Cost of Bat} = 1.10
\]
\[
x + (x + 1) = 1.10
\]

**Simplify and solve for \( x \):**
\[
2x + 1 = 1.10
\]
\[
2x = 1.10 - 1
\]
\[
2x = 0.10
\]
\[
x = \frac{0.10}{2}
\]
\[
x = 0.05
\]

**Therefore, the ball costs:**
\[
\boxed{0.05}
\]
