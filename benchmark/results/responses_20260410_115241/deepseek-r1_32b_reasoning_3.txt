Prompt: A lily pad doubles in size every day. It takes 48 days to cover a lake. How long does it take to cover half the lake? Show your working.
========================================
**Solution:**

We are given that a lily pad doubles in size every day and takes **48 days** to completely cover a lake. We need to determine how long it takes for the lily pad to cover **half** the lake.

1. **Understanding the Growth Pattern:**
   
   Since the lily pad **doubles** its size each day, the growth follows an exponential pattern. This means that on any given day, the lily pad is twice as large as it was the previous day.

2. **Relating Days to Coverage:**
   
   - On **Day 48**, the lily pad covers the entire lake.
   - Since it doubles each day, the coverage on **Day 47** would be half of what it is on Day 48.

3. **Calculating Half Coverage:**
   
   \[
   \text{Coverage on Day } (n-1) = \frac{\text{Coverage on Day } n}{2}
   \]
   
   Applying this to our scenario:
   
   \[
   \text{Coverage on Day 47} = \frac{\text{Coverage on Day 48}}{2} = \frac{100\%}{2} = 50\%
   \]

**Answer:**

It takes \(\boxed{47}\) days for the lily pad to cover half of the lake.
