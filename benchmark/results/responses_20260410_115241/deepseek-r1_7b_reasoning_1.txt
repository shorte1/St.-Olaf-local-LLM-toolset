Prompt: A bat and a ball cost $1.10 in total. The bat costs $1.00 more than the ball. How much does the ball cost? Show your working.
========================================
Let's solve the problem step by step.

**Step 1: Define Variables**
- Let \( b \) represent the cost of the ball in dollars.
- The bat costs \$1.00 more than the ball, so its cost is \( b + 1.00 \).

**Step 2: Set Up the Equation**
According to the problem, the total cost of the bat and the ball is \$1.10:
\[
b + (b + 1.00) = 1.10
\]

**Step 3: Combine Like Terms**
Combine \( b \) and \( b \):
\[
2b + 1.00 = 1.10
\]

**Step 4: Solve for \( b \)**
Subtract \$1.00 from both sides of the equation:
\[
2b = 1.10 - 1.00
\]
\[
2b = 0.10
\]
Divide both sides by 2 to find \( b \):
\[
b = \frac{0.10}{2}
\]
\[
b = 0.05
\]

**Conclusion:**
The ball costs \(\boxed{\$0.05}\).
