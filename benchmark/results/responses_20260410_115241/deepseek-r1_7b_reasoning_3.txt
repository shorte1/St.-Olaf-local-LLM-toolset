Prompt: A lily pad doubles in size every day. It takes 48 days to cover a lake. How long does it take to cover half the lake? Show your working.
========================================
The problem involves a lily pad that doubles in size every day and covers a lake completely in 48 days. We need to determine how long it takes for the lily pad to cover half the lake.

1. Denote the size of the lily pad on day \( n \) as \( L(n) \).
2. The lily pad doubles in size each day, so \( L(n + 1) = 2 \times L(n) \).
3. It takes 48 days to cover the entire lake, thus \( L(48) = \text{Lake} \).
4. Since the lily pad doubles every day, on day 47, its size would be half of what it is on day 48.
5. Therefore, \( L(47) = \frac{\text{Lake}}{2} \).

Hence, it takes 47 days for the lily pad to cover half the lake.
