Prompt: You have two ropes that each take exactly one hour to burn, but they burn unevenly. How do you measure exactly 45 minutes using only these ropes and a lighter?
========================================
To measure exactly 45 minutes using two ropes that each take exactly one hour to burn (but not necessarily evenly), we can follow these steps:

1. **Light both ends of Rope A** and one end of Rope B simultaneously at time zero.
   - Rope A, being lit from both ends, will burn out in 30 minutes.
   - Rope B, being lit from one end, will have been burning for 30 minutes when Rope A is completely burned.

2. **When Rope A is completely burned (after 30 minutes)**:
   - Immediately light the other end of Rope B.

3. **Rope B will now burn out in an additional 15 minutes** because it was already burning from one end for 30 minutes and has 30 minutes left to burn when lit from both ends, which takes half the time (15 minutes).

Adding these intervals together:
- 30 minutes (for Rope A) + 15 minutes (remaining time for Rope B) = 45 minutes.

Thus, the exact 45-minute measurement is achieved.
