Prompt: Write a haiku about autumn, then translate it to French, then explain the seasonal symbolism used.
========================================
Here is the haiku:

Golden leaves descend
Crisp air whispers farewell cold
Autumn's gentle sigh

Translation to French:
Feuilles d'or s'abattent
Vent froide chuchote adieu froid
Sillage doux de l'automne

Explanation of seasonal symbolism:

In this haiku, the speaker captures the essence of autumn with its characteristic imagery and tone.

* "Golden leaves descend" (Feuilles d'or s'abattent) symbolizes the fall of leaves, a common phenomenon during autumn. The use of "golden" specifically highlights the vibrant colors of the season.
* "Crisp air whispers farewell cold" (Vent froide chuchote adieu froid) conveys the invigorating chill of autumn's arrival, as well as the sense of departure and goodbye to warmer seasons.
* "Autumn's gentle sigh" (Sillage doux de l'automne) evokes a sense of melancholy and nostalgia, suggesting that autumn is a time for quiet reflection and letting go.

The haiku's imagery and tone work together to evoke a sense of the season as a moment of transition, one that marks the end of warmth and vitality with the arrival of cooler temperatures and the fading light. The use of "gentle sigh" in particular suggests a peaceful, almost mournful quality, underscoring the idea that autumn is a time for introspection and release.
