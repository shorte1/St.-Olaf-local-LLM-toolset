Prompt: A bat and a ball cost $1.10 in total. The bat costs $1.00 more than the ball. How much does the ball cost? Show your working.
========================================
Let's break this down step by step:

Let x be the cost of the ball.

Since the bat costs $1.00 more than the ball, the cost of the bat is x + 1.

The total cost of the bat and the ball together is $1.10.

We can set up an equation to represent this situation:

x (cost of the ball) + (x + 1) (cost of the bat) = 1.10

Combine like terms:

2x + 1 = 1.10

Subtract 1 from both sides:

2x = 0.10

Divide by 2:

x = 0.05

So, the ball costs $0.05.

Let's check our answer:

If the bat costs $1.00 more than the ball, and the ball costs $0.05, then the bat costs $1.00 + $0.05 = $1.05.
The total cost of the bat and the ball together is indeed $1.10 ($1.05 + $0.05).
