Prompt: You have two ropes that each take exactly one hour to burn, but they burn unevenly. How do you measure exactly 45 minutes using only these ropes and a lighter?
========================================
This is a classic problem. Here's the solution:

1. Light both ends of one rope (let's call it Rope A).
2. When the entire Rope A has burned down, that takes 1 hour.
3. At this point, take the other rope (Rope B) and light its top end only.
4. Now, you have two burning ropes: the bottom half of Rope A (which will burn for another 30 minutes, since it's still burning from lighting both ends initially) and the entire length of Rope B.

To measure exactly 45 minutes, follow these steps:

1. Wait until Rope B has burned down completely (taking approximately 45-50 minutes to do so, depending on how unevenly it burns).
2. At this point, you have only the bottom half of Rope A left burning.
3. Since Rope A takes another 30 minutes to burn from lighting both ends initially, this remaining burning time will be enough to measure exactly 15 minutes (30 - 45 = 15).

So, you've effectively measured out 45 minutes using these two ropes and a lighter!
