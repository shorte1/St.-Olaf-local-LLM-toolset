Prompt: A bat and a ball cost $1.10 in total. The bat costs $1.00 more than the ball. How much does the ball cost? Show your working.
========================================
 Let's denote the cost of the ball as \( x \) dollars. According to the problem, the bat costs $1.00 more than the ball, so the cost of the bat is \( x + 1 \).

The total cost of the bat and the ball is given as $1.10, so we can write the equation:

\[ x + (x + 1) = 1.10 \]

Now, let's solve for \( x \):

\[ 2x + 1 = 1.10 \]

Subtract 1 from both sides:

\[ 2x = 1.10 - 1 \]
\[ 2x = 0.10 \]

Divide both sides by 2:

\[ x = \frac{0.10}{2} \]
\[ x = 0.05 \]

Therefore, the cost of the ball is $0.05 (or 5 cents).
