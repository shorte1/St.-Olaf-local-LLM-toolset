========================================
 Here is a list of the planets in our solar system, arranged from largest to smallest by diameter, along with their diameters (in kilometers) and whether they have rings:

1. Jupiter (139,820 km) - Gas giant with prominent ring system
2. Saturn (116,460 km) - Gas giant with extensive ring system
3. Uranus (50,724 km) - Ice giant, no visible rings
4. Neptune (49,244 km) - Ice giant, no visible rings
5. Earth (12,742 km)
6. Venus (12,104 km)
7. Mars (6,779 km)
8. Mercury (4,879 km)

It's important to note that Pluto is not considered a planet in the solar system by the International Astronomical Union (IAU). If we were to include it, its diameter would be approximately 11,720 kilometers, and it does not have visible rings.
