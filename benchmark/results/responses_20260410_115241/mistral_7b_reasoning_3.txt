Prompt: A lily pad doubles in size every day. It takes 48 days to cover a lake. How long does it take to cover half the lake? Show your working.
========================================
 Let's break this down:

1. The lily pad doubles in size each day, so we can express the area (A) covered by the lily pad on the nth day as 2^n square units.

2. On the 48th day, the entire lake (L) is covered, which means A = L. So, we have the equation: 2^{48} = L.

3. Let H be half of the original lake's area, so H = L/2. Now we want to find out when the lily pad covers an area equal to H (half of the lake).

4. The time it takes for the lily pad to cover H can be found by solving the equation 2^n = H (since A = 2^n and H < L, implying that the lake is not fully covered yet).

5. Taking the logarithm base 2 of both sides gives us n = log_2(H).

6. To find out how many days it takes for the lily pad to cover half the lake, we calculate the number of days needed: n = log_2(L/2) = log_2(L) - log_2(2) = log_2(L) - 1 (since log_2(2) = 1).

7. Since we know that 2^48 covers the entire lake, log_2(L) = 48, so n = 48 - 1 = 47 days.

Therefore, it takes 47 days for the lily pad to cover half of the lake.
