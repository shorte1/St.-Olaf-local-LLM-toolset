Prompt: Write a haiku about autumn, then translate it to French, then explain the seasonal symbolism used.
========================================
Haiku:
Leaves whisper softly,
Crisp air holds secrets of smoke—
Sunset paints gold hues.

French translation:
Feuilles murmurent doucement,
L'air frais garde des secrets de fumée—
Coucher de soleil peint des teintes dorées.

Explanation of seasonal symbolism:

1. "Leaves whisper softly" - This line symbolizes the gentle rustling sound that leaves make as they dry out and fall from trees in autumn. The softness represents the transition period, where nature begins to quiet down after the vibrancy of summer.

2. "Crisp air holds secrets of smoke"— Here, crisp air signifies the cooler temperatures that come with autumn. The "secrets of smoke" can symbolize the bonfires or campfires often associated with this season, as well as the scent of woodsmoke from chimneys as people start to heat their homes for the approaching colder months.

3. "Sunset paints gold hues"— This line references the warm golden sunsets that are common in autumn when the shorter days and changing light conditions create stunningly colorful skies at dusk, reminiscent of the golds and reds seen in fall foliage.
