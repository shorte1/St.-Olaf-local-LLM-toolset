Prompt: What were the main causes and consequences of the 2008 financial crisis?
========================================
The 2008 financial crisis was one of the most significant economic downturns since the Great Depression. It had profound impacts on global economies, financial markets, and individual households. Understanding its causes and consequences is essential for comprehending modern economic policies and market behaviors.

### Causes:
1. **Subprime Mortgages**: A substantial factor leading to the crisis was the widespread issuance of subprime mortgages—loans given to borrowers with poor credit histories at higher interest rates. These loans were often made under relaxed lending standards, assuming that rising housing prices would cover any potential losses.
   
2. **Securitization**: Banks and financial institutions bundled these risky mortgages into mortgage-backed securities (MBS) and collateralized debt obligations (CDOs), which they sold to investors around the world. This process spread risk beyond traditional banking markets, making it difficult for individuals or companies to assess their exposure accurately.

3. **Housing Bubble**: The U.S. housing market experienced a significant bubble as prices soared far above sustainable levels, fueled by easy credit and speculative buying. When this bubble burst in 2006-2007, it triggered widespread defaults on subprime mortgages.

4. **Financial Engineering**: Complex financial instruments created to profit from these conditions also contributed to the crisis. Instruments like CDOs and derivatives were often misunderstood even by those selling them, leading to mispricing of risk.
   
5. **Lack of Regulation**: Regulatory bodies failed to adequately address the risks posed by these new financial instruments and practices. There was a lack of oversight on both subprime lending and the securitization process.

### Consequences:
1. **Global Recession**: The collapse in credit markets led to a severe global economic downturn, with significant GDP contractions across major economies. Unemployment rates rose sharply worldwide.
   
2. **Banking System Collapse**: Many banks failed or required bailouts from governments around the world due to their exposure to toxic assets and defaults. This led to consolidation within the banking sector.
   
3. **Government Interventions**: Governments and central banks responded with unprecedented measures, including large-scale quantitative easing (QE) programs and direct fiscal stimulus to stabilize economies and markets.
   
4. **Reform in Financial Regulation**: The crisis prompted significant regulatory reforms, such as the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act in the U.S., aimed at increasing oversight of financial institutions and improving consumer protection.

5. **Impact on Individuals**: Millions lost their homes due to foreclosures, while others faced unemployment or underemployment. The crisis also led to a loss of confidence among consumers and investors, affecting spending and investment patterns for years afterward.

In summary, the 2008 financial crisis was triggered by complex interactions involving lax lending standards, securitization of subprime mortgages, and insufficient regulatory oversight. Its consequences were far-reaching, impacting economic stability globally and prompting major changes in financial regulation and market practices.
