Prompt: A bat and a ball cost $1.10 in total. The bat costs $1.00 more than the ball. How much does the ball cost? Show your working.
========================================
Let's denote the cost of the ball as \( x \) dollars.

According to the problem, the bat costs $1.00 more than the ball. Therefore, the cost of the bat can be expressed as \( x + 1.00 \) dollars.

The total cost of both items is given as $1.10. Thus, we have:

\[ x + (x + 1.00) = 1.10 \]

Simplifying this equation:
\[ 2x + 1.00 = 1.10 \]

Subtract 1.00 from both sides to isolate the term with \( x \):
\[ 2x = 0.10 \]

Divide by 2 to solve for \( x \):
\[ x = 0.05 \]

Therefore, the ball costs $0.05 (or 5 cents).

To verify:
- The cost of the ball is $0.05.
- The bat then costs $1.00 more than the ball: \( 0.05 + 1.00 = 1.05 \).
- Together, they total: \( 0.05 + 1.05 = 1.10 \).

The solution is correct; the ball costs **$0.05**.
