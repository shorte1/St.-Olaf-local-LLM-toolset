Prompt: If it takes 5 machines 5 minutes to make 5 widgets, how long would it take 100 machines to make 100 widgets? Show your working.
========================================
To solve this problem, we need to understand the relationship between the number of machines, time taken, and the number of widgets produced.

Given:
- 5 machines can produce 5 widgets in 5 minutes.

First, let's find out how many widgets one machine can produce in that given time.
- Since 5 machines make 5 widgets in 5 minutes,
- One machine makes \( \frac{5}{5} = 1 \) widget in 5 minutes.

Next, we need to figure out the production rate of one machine:
- Therefore, one machine produces 1 widget every 5 minutes.

Now, let's determine how long it would take for 100 machines to produce 100 widgets.
- If each machine makes 1 widget in 5 minutes,
- Then 100 machines will make \( 100 \times 1 = 100 \) widgets in the same amount of time (since all machines are working simultaneously).

Thus, it would take:
\[ 5 \text{ minutes} \]

So, it would take 5 minutes for 100 machines to produce 100 widgets.
