Prompt: List the planets in our solar system by size from largest to smallest, give their diameter in km, and mark which ones have rings.
========================================
Sure! Here is the list of the planets in our solar system ordered from largest to smallest based on their diameters, along with their respective sizes in kilometers (km) and a note if they have rings:

1. **Jupiter** - Diameter: 139,820 km (largest planet; has rings)
2. **Saturn** - Diameter: 116,460 km (has rings)
3. **Uranus** - Diameter: 50,724 km (has rings)
4. **Neptune** - Diameter: 49,244 km
5. **Earth** - Diameter: 12,742 km
6. **Mars** - Diameter: 6,779 km
7. **Juno** - Diameter: 10,892 km (though not a ringed planet)
8. **Venus** - Diameter: 12,104 km
9. **Mercury** - Diameter: 4,880 km (smallest terrestrial planet)

Note that Pluto, which used to be classified as the ninth planet, is now considered a dwarf planet and does not have rings. The order of Juno was added at the end as it's sometimes listed between Earth and Mars in discussions about relative sizes due to its rocky composition; however, it is not a ringed planet.

The gas giants Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune all possess ring systems.
