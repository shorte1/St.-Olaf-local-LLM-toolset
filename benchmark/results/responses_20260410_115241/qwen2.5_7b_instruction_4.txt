**Chocolate Chip Cookies**

*Prep Time: 20 minutes | Cook Time: 12-15 minutes*

### Ingredients:
- 3 and 1/4 cups (450g) all-purpose flour
- 1 tsp baking soda
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 cup unsalted butter, softened (2 sticks or 227g)
- 3/4 cup white sugar (6 ounces or 170g)
- 3/4 cups brown sugar (6 ounces or 170g)
- 2 large eggs
- 2 tsp vanilla extract
- 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips (8 ounces or 227g)

### Steps:
1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt; set aside.
3. In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened butter, white sugar, and brown sugar until creamy and well combined.
4. Add in the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition.
5. Stir in the vanilla extract.
6. Gradually mix in the flour mixture from step 2 into the butter mixture, stirring until just combined to avoid overmixing.
7. Fold in the chocolate chips evenly throughout the dough.
8. Using a spoon or cookie scoop, drop rounded balls of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart (about 1 tablespoon for smaller cookies).
9. Bake in the preheated oven for 12 to 15 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden and the centers are set but still soft.
10. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Enjoy your homemade chocolate chip cookies!
