Prompt: A bat and a ball cost $1.10 in total. The bat costs $1.00 more than the ball. How much does the ball cost? Show your working.
========================================
Let's denote the cost of the ball as \( x \) dollars.

According to the problem, the bat costs $1.00 more than the ball. Therefore, the cost of the bat can be expressed as \( x + 1 \) dollars.

The total cost of the bat and the ball is given as $1.10. So we can write the equation:

\[ x + (x + 1) = 1.10 \]

Simplifying this equation:

\[ 2x + 1 = 1.10 \]

Subtract 1 from both sides:

\[ 2x = 0.10 \]

Now, divide by 2:

\[ x = 0.05 \]

So, the ball costs $0.05 (or 5 cents).

To verify, if the ball costs $0.05 and the bat costs $1.05 more than the ball, then the bat costs:

\[ 0.05 + 1.00 = 1.05 \]

Adding these together gives:

\[ 0.05 + 1.05 = 1.10 \]

This confirms that our solution is correct. The ball costs $0.05.
